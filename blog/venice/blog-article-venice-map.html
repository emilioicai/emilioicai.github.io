Is it Venice Really an island?

In fact Venice is a set of islands to be presice, the city is made out of 118 islands linked by 430 bridges.
venice is on top of a salt lake, separated for the adriatic sea by another island named Lido, this fact is really important in the city's origins because in the year 421 the italian peninsula got invaded by many civilizations which forced the inhabitants of many cities to escape their home towns searching for safer places. The people living in the Mestre area used the salt lake for fishing and knew it very well so when the civilization attacked, they decided to move to the salt lake and build their houses there, far of the reach of the enemies army. But what about of the enemies boat? ..well the whole lake was packed with natural canals which served as a defence sistem for the city, all the canals in venice range from 0.5 meters to 2 meters deep which prevented boats for sailing inside of the city unless you knew very well the place in advance. This natural defence sistem prevented the enemies of the city from invade it for more than 1200 years.
The first person to successfully invide venice was Napoleon in 1797, before that the city was totally safe making it extremally prosperous, but not all canal are so low there are two deep canals in the city the grand canal with 5 to 8 meters deep and the giudecca canal with almost 15 meters deep, both where artificially made so larger boats could came into de city
